Hub of the Americas

"A man, a plan, a canal: Panama."

Many countries in the region offer beaches, rainforests, mountains, modern cities and bucolic villages. Panama, aside from all these, offers a premium no other country in the region can: it is a recognized air, maritime and communications hub — literally the Hub of the Americas. Its location at the center of the Americas connects 90+ cities in Europe and America with daily direct non-stop flights. Five percent of all global maritime trade navigates through here. So whatever can't be sourced locally, it can be transported here — fast.

Panama's versatility as a location has been proven widely. The Bond franchise used it to play Bolivia and Haiti in Quantum of Solace; The Suicide Squad turned a derelict corner of Colón into the South American island of Corto Maltese; in Prison Break and Contraband, Panama plays itself. Its compact size and modern roads move productions fast — from the Caribbean to the Pacific in a little over an hour (82 km). No wonder it has the fastest transcontinental railroad.

Carbon Negative

One of three carbon-negative countries on Earth — and the only 100% solar studio in it.

Along with Bhutan and Suriname, Panama is one of just three carbon-negative countries in the world. Verite Producciones is a local leader in environmental production, operating the only 100% solar-powered studio facility in the country. We help visiting productions shrink their footprint — reusable crew bottles instead of single-use plastic, composting and biodegradable catering on remote islands and villages, and full compliance with Panama's laws protecting its flora and fauna.

Discretion

Some productions can't afford a leak.

High-profile talent, unannounced projects, competitive campaigns — we treat confidentiality as infrastructure, not a favour. Every crew member signs an NDA before the first call sheet. Closed sets, controlled access, secure transport and locked location lists keep your project invisible until you choose otherwise.

And the country itself is built for it: private islands, remote jungle, and controlled points of entry. In Panama, what shoots here stays here.

Dry Season
Late December to April — the prime window. El Niño and La Niña aside, reliable sun and calm seas.
Green Season
Rains from early May to mid-December (September–October the wettest). Downpours are fast and furious, and can turn a somber sky into a bright day — with the magic-hour sunset that follows.
Daylight & Time
Near the Equator: an almost even 12 hours of daylight. Upper-20s to mid-30s °C, humidity in the mid-80s. GMT−5.
